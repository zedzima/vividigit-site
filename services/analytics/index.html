WHY ANALYTICS MATTERS

Every Marketing Decision Is a Guess Until You Fix the Data

Without accurate tracking and attribution, you are optimizing based on numbers that are wrong. Broken analytics does not just waste reporting time — it misallocates budget, overstates channel performance, and hides the real drivers of revenue.

Misconfigured tracking costs more than no tracking at all

A GA4 setup that double-fires events, misses consent states, or loses cross-domain sessions produces data that looks plausible but is factually wrong. Teams make budget decisions based on these numbers every month. Fixing the foundation is not a nice-to-have — it is a prerequisite for every other marketing investment.

1

Attribution models determine where budget goes

Last-click attribution credits the final touchpoint and ignores everything that built demand. First-click ignores everything that closed the deal. Multi-touch attribution assigns proportional credit across the real buyer journey — so you can scale what actually works instead of what happens to be last in the chain.

2

Dashboards should drive decisions, not decorate walls

A dashboard that shows 40 metrics helps nobody. Effective reporting surfaces the five to ten KPIs your team needs to act on this week, segmented by channel, campaign, and audience. If your dashboard does not change behavior, it is not a dashboard — it is decoration.

3

ANALYTICS FAQ

Questions Buyers Ask About Analytics

Which analytics tools do you work with?

We specialize in GA4, Google Tag Manager, Looker Studio, and BigQuery. We also work with Matomo, Plausible, Mixpanel, Amplitude, Power BI, and Tableau depending on your stack and requirements. Tool selection is based on your needs, not our preferences.

How do you handle data privacy and GDPR compliance?

Privacy is built into every implementation. We configure consent-aware tracking, implement server-side solutions where needed, and ensure compliance with GDPR, ePrivacy, and other regulations. Every setup includes proper consent management integration and data retention policies.

What is the difference between analytics setup and attribution modeling?

Analytics setup is the foundation: making sure events fire correctly, data is clean, and dashboards show accurate numbers. Attribution modeling is the layer on top: assigning credit across marketing touchpoints to understand which channels and campaigns actually drive conversions. You need the first before the second is meaningful.

How long does an analytics implementation take?

An analytics audit takes 5-7 business days. A full GA4 setup with custom dashboards typically takes 2-4 weeks. Attribution modeling requires 4-6 weeks of clean data collection before the model can be validated. We phase the work so you see value at each stage.

What is the best first step?

An analytics audit: we review your current GA4 setup, tag management, event architecture, and data quality. It shows exactly what is broken, what is missing, and which fixes will have the biggest impact on data accuracy and decision-making.
