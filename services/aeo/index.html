AEO

AI-Powered Search Is Already Redirecting Traffic Away From Traditional Results

ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews answer user queries directly. If your brand is not in those answers, the visibility goes to competitors who are.

LLMs do not rank pages — they cite sources they can parse

Traditional SEO optimizes for crawlers and ranking algorithms. AEO optimizes for language models that extract, summarize, and attribute information. The content formats, entity signals, and authority markers that LLMs rely on are different from what Google's crawler evaluates. Structured data, clear entity definitions, and consistent cross-references matter more than keyword density.

1

AI visibility compounds across platforms, not just one search engine

When your content is properly structured for LLM consumption, it becomes citable by ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude, and Microsoft Copilot simultaneously. A single optimization effort propagates across every AI platform that ingests your content — unlike traditional SEO, where each search engine has its own ranking factors.

2

Waiting means losing ground to competitors who move first

AI models are trained and updated on fixed schedules. Content that establishes entity authority and citation patterns now gets embedded in model weights during the next training cycle. Brands that delay optimization miss those windows and have to compete against incumbents who are already established in LLM knowledge.

3
WHAT AEO COVERS

What AI Engine Optimization Includes

LLM content optimization

Restructuring your content so language models can accurately extract, summarize, and cite your brand. This means clear entity definitions, FAQ schemas, and answer-ready formatting — not just adding keywords.

AI Overview and featured snippet targeting

Optimizing page structure, schema markup, and content format to appear in Google AI Overviews and featured snippet positions where AI-enhanced search results are displayed.

Entity SEO and knowledge graph signals

Building unambiguous entity identity through structured data, knowledge panels, Wikipedia and Wikidata presence, and authoritative cross-references that LLMs use to verify and attribute information.

AEO FAQ

Questions Buyers Ask About AEO

How is AEO different from SEO?

SEO optimizes for search engine ranking algorithms. AEO optimizes for large language models that extract and cite information. The tactics differ: AEO focuses on entity clarity, structured data, and content formats that LLMs can parse, while SEO prioritizes keywords, backlinks, and technical crawlability. The best results come from combining both.

How long does AEO take to show results?

Content and schema changes can affect AI Overviews within weeks. LLM citation changes depend on model retraining schedules — typically 2-4 months for platforms like ChatGPT and Perplexity. Early movers benefit because entity authority established before a training cycle gets embedded in model weights.

Which AI platforms do you optimize for?

ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Gemini, Claude, and Microsoft Copilot. Each platform has different citation patterns and data sources, so we tailor the strategy per platform rather than applying a single template.

Can you guarantee AI citations for my brand?

No. LLM outputs are probabilistic and change with each model update. What we deliver is a systematic approach that maximizes citation probability: proper entity signals, structured content, and authoritative sourcing that AI models prefer to reference.

What is the best first step?

An AI Visibility Audit: we check how major AI platforms currently cite your brand, where competitors appear instead, and which content and entity changes will have the highest impact. It gives you a clear picture before committing to a larger engagement.
