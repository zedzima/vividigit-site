WHY EMAIL MATTERS

Email Delivers the Highest ROI — When It Actually Reaches the Inbox

Email averages $36 return per $1 spent. But broken deliverability, shallow segmentation, and missing automation silently kill that potential.

Deliverability is the foundation — everything else depends on it

If your emails land in spam or the Promotions tab, open rates collapse and revenue disappears. SPF, DKIM, DMARC, sender reputation, list hygiene — these are not optional technical details. They are the foundation that determines whether your email program generates revenue or burns budget.

1

Lifecycle sequences outperform broadcast campaigns

Welcome series, abandoned cart flows, post-purchase sequences, and win-back campaigns generate more revenue per email than any promotional blast. The difference is timing and relevance — automated sequences send the right message when the subscriber is most likely to act, not when your marketing calendar says so.

2

Segmentation turns a list into a revenue asset

Sending the same email to every subscriber is the fastest way to increase unsubscribes and decrease engagement. Behavioral segmentation, purchase history, engagement scoring, and lifecycle stage determine who gets what message. A well-segmented list of 10,000 outperforms an unsegmented list of 100,000.

EMAIL MARKETING FAQ

Questions Buyers Ask About Email Marketing

Which email service provider (ESP) do you work with?

We work with all major ESPs including Klaviyo, Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, HubSpot, Brevo, and ConvertKit. If you already have a platform, we work within it. If you are choosing one, we recommend the best fit based on your list size, features needed, and budget.

How long does it take to see results from email marketing?

Deliverability fixes show impact within days. Automation sequences typically generate measurable revenue within 2-4 weeks of launch. Full email program optimization — segmentation, lifecycle sequences, and ongoing campaigns — compounds over 3-6 months as list quality and engagement improve.

Is there a minimum list size to get started?

No minimum list size is required. For lists under 1,000 subscribers, we typically recommend starting with a deliverability audit and automation setup rather than full campaign management. The value of ongoing management scales with your audience size.

How do you handle GDPR and email compliance?

Compliance is built into everything we do. We ensure proper consent collection, manage preference centers, include required unsubscribe mechanisms, and maintain clean suppression lists. We advise on GDPR, CAN-SPAM, and CASL requirements specific to your markets.

What is the best first step?

A deliverability audit: authentication check, sender reputation analysis, inbox placement testing, and list health assessment. It shows exactly where email revenue is being lost and which fixes will have the biggest impact.
