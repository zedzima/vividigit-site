TYPES OF SCOPES

Four types of scope of work — depending on where you are

Not every engagement is the same. Scopes of work range from diagnostic first steps to ongoing management layers.

Diagnostic and audit packages

The best first step when priorities are unclear. A digital marketing audit produces a structured assessment with evidence-based recommendations — not a generic checklist. The lowest-cost entry point.

  • + Technical SEO audit, AI visibility review, analytics health check
  • + Clear findings with prioritized action items
  • + Decision-ready output for the next step

Setup and implementation scopes

When the diagnosis is done and the fix is clear. Setup scopes of work handle the implementation: tracking configuration, platform migration, content architecture, automation workflows.

  • + Defined implementation tasks with QA gates
  • + Documentation and handoff included
  • + Technical work delivered by named experts

Campaign and pilot scopes

When the system is ready and you want to test the execution model. Pilot scopes of work run a controlled first campaign or content cycle with measurable targets.

  • + Time-boxed engagement with defined KPIs
  • + Real execution, not a simulation
  • + Evidence for the scale decision

Ongoing management scopes

When the first scopes prove the model and you want continuous execution. Management scopes of work provide ongoing expert capacity with regular deliverables and transparent reporting.

  • + Monthly or quarterly engagement cycles
  • + Visible progress and decision checkpoints
  • + Scale up or down based on results

STILL HAVE QUESTIONS?

Questions about digital marketing packages and pricing

How packages work, what they cost, and how to choose.

How much do digital marketing packages cost?

Digital marketing pricing depends on the scope: diagnostic audits start at a few hundred dollars, setup and implementation packages range from $500 to $3,000, and ongoing management scopes are priced monthly based on channels and markets. Every package has transparent pricing visible before you commit — no hidden fees, no retainer surprises. Digital marketing costs are always defined upfront.

What is the difference between a package and a service?

A service is the marketing direction — like SEO, PPC, or Content. A package is a specific engagement inside that service: a technical audit, a campaign setup, an ongoing management layer. Services organize packages; packages are what you actually buy. Each digital marketing package has defined tasks, deliverables, and pricing.

What is a digital marketing audit?

A digital marketing audit is a diagnostic package that assesses your current state — technical SEO health, analytics setup, content performance, competitive landscape, or AI visibility. It produces a structured assessment with evidence-based recommendations and a prioritized action plan. Audits are the lowest-risk first engagement and the fastest way to identify where to invest next.

Can I start with just one package?

Yes, and that is how most engagements begin. A diagnostic audit or a focused setup package is usually the lowest-risk first step. It produces clarity and naturally leads to broader packages if the results support it. There is no minimum commitment beyond the single package you choose.

Can I combine multiple digital marketing packages?

Yes. You can combine packages from the same service or across services. For common combinations — like SEO audit plus content setup, or PPC campaign plus analytics implementation — solutions package them automatically with strategic coordination. For custom needs, we scope the combination during a consultation.

Do you offer digital marketing agency packages for international markets?

Yes. Every package can be executed in 17+ languages with native experts per market. Multilingual packages include market-specific keyword research, localized content, and per-country campaign management. International pricing scales with the number of markets and languages in scope — all defined transparently upfront.
