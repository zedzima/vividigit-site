COMPENSATION MODEL

You earn based on the value you create

Not a salary. Not an hourly rate. A transparent share of the revenue your work generates.

Revenue Share

Experts receive a 40% core share of the revenue they generate. Final payout depends on scope, rate, delivered units, and contribution. Transparent and directly linked to real deliverables.

  • No hidden margins

Profit Participation

20% of company profit is distributed annually across the team. Allocation is unit-based: contribution, responsibility, impact, and ownership mindset determine your share.

  • 20% of distributable profit
  • Unit-based allocation
  • Annual bonus distribution

Phantom Equity

Key contributors receive long-term financial upside aligned with company growth. No shareholder complexity, no legal overhead. Your incentives grow with the system.

  • Long-term upside
  • Impact-based allocation
  • Aligned with company growth

STILL HAVE QUESTIONS?

Common questions about digital marketing jobs at Vividigit

How the model works, what to expect, and how to join.

What is a productized services agency and how is it different from a normal agency?

A productized services agency packages expert work into defined modules with clear scope, deliverables, and quality standards — instead of selling hours or vague retainers. For you as an expert, this means the work arrives structured: you know what you own, what the quality bar is, and how your output connects to revenue. You are not filling time sheets. You are delivering defined outcomes through a shared operating system that handles coordination, billing, and quality control. The difference from a traditional agency is that you earn from what you produce, not from what someone decides to pay you.

How do I get matched to projects and clients?

When a client engagement requires your market, language, industry, or service expertise, you get matched to a defined scope inside that engagement. The system tracks which experts cover which capabilities, so matching happens through the delivery architecture — not through random assignment or internal politics. You can be matched to multiple scopes across different clients simultaneously, which is how earnings scale. If a scope requires your specific market knowledge — say, German SERPs or French regulatory nuance — you are the operator for that module, coordinated through shared SOPs with the rest of the team.

What does a typical work week look like?

It depends on how many active scopes you are matched to. Some experts work 10 hours per week on one focused scope. Others work 30+ hours across several markets or services. The work itself is structured: you receive a defined module with clear deliverables, use shared templates and SOPs, collaborate through documented workflows, and submit through quality gates. There are no daily standups for the sake of standups. Communication is tied to real decisions and deliverables. Most experts say they spend more time on actual expert work here than they did in any previous agency or in-house role.

What kinds of digital marketing jobs are available?

Service leads for SEO, analytics, content, PPC, and AI visibility. Scope experts for technical SEO, link building, on-page optimization, or localization workflows. Country and language managers who understand local buyer behavior, SERPs, and cultural context. Industry-specific operators for healthcare, SaaS, ecommerce, fintech, and other verticals where domain knowledge is critical. If you can own a capability in a specific market, language, or vertical, there is likely a role — even if it is not listed yet.

Are these full-time digital marketing jobs or project-based roles?

Most roles are project-based, part-time, or fractional. We match experts to defined scopes and market-specific workstreams rather than defaulting to full-time staffing. Some roles grow into ongoing retainer relationships as the work expands. The model is designed so you can work across multiple clients and markets simultaneously — which is both more interesting and more lucrative than a single full-time position.

How does revenue share work at a marketing agency?

Core compensation is a 40% revenue share on the revenue your work generates. Final payout depends on scope, rate, delivered units, and contribution. This is not a bonus on top of a low base — it is the primary compensation model. On top of that, 20% of company profit is distributed annually through a unit-based Profit Participation Pool. Key contributors also receive phantom equity for long-term upside. The economics are transparent by design: you can trace your payout to specific deliverables.

What is the salary range for fractional marketing experts?

There is no fixed salary. Earnings are tied to revenue share, so they scale with the volume and value of work you deliver. An expert working across multiple scopes and markets can earn significantly more than a comparable full-time marketing salary — because earnings are not capped by salary bands or annual review cycles. The more scopes you can own confidently, the higher your earnings potential.

Can I work remotely from any country?

Yes, the operating model is remote-first by design. What matters is your expertise, time zone overlap with active projects, language ability, and delivery discipline — not your office location. We work with experts across Europe, North America, and other regions. Specific projects may require overlap with certain time zones, but there is no blanket location requirement.

Do I need to match an exact listed vacancy?

No. If your expertise clearly maps to one of our services, markets, languages, or industries, reach out even if your exact role is not listed yet. The system expands as new verticals and markets are added. Many of the strongest expert relationships started from an inbound introduction that did not match any open listing at the time.
