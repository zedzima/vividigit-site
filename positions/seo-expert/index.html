ROLE OVERVIEW

What You Will Actually Do

Market-level SEO execution, not generic international strategy.

Responsibilities

You take ownership of defined SEO work inside a specific market or language context.

  • Keyword research Market-specific keyword analysis, search demand mapping, and SERP review for your target market
  • On-page optimization Localization-aware recommendations for title tags, content structure, internal linking, and metadata
  • Technical SEO input Audit contributions where local search behavior, crawl patterns, or rendering matter
  • Competitor analysis Regular monitoring of competitors in your market — rankings, content strategy, link profiles
  • Content briefs Search-driven briefs for content teams based on keyword gaps and SERP opportunities
  • Reporting Market-level performance reporting through shared dashboards and quality gates

Requirements

What we need from you to be effective in this role.

  • SEO experience 5+ years of hands-on work across technical, on-page, or international SEO
  • Market knowledge Real familiarity with at least one market, language, or buyer context
  • Tools proficiency Confident with Google Search Console, GA4, Screaming Frog, Ahrefs or Semrush
  • Ownership mindset Readiness to own outcomes for your scope, not just execute isolated tasks
  • Communication Clear written communication in English; working language of your market is a plus

Nice to have

Not required, but these make you a stronger fit.

  • Multiple markets Experience across more than one country or language cluster
  • Industry depth Domain expertise in SaaS, ecommerce, healthcare, or other verticals we serve
  • Link building Hands-on experience with outreach, digital PR, or link acquisition

STILL HAVE QUESTIONS?

Common questions about SEO expert jobs at Vividigit

How the role works, what to expect, and how compensation is structured.

How do SEO experts get matched to projects and clients?

When a client engagement requires your market or language expertise, you get matched to a defined SEO scope inside that engagement. The system tracks which experts cover which markets and capabilities, so matching happens through the delivery architecture — not random assignment. You can work on multiple scopes across different clients simultaneously, which is how earnings scale. If a scope needs German SERP knowledge or French regulatory nuance, you are the operator for that module.

What does a typical work week look like for a remote SEO expert here?

It depends on active scopes. Some experts work 10 hours per week on one focused market. Others work 30+ hours across several. The work is structured: you receive a module with clear deliverables, use shared templates and SOPs, and submit through quality gates. No daily standups for the sake of standups. Communication is tied to real decisions. Most SEO experts say they spend more time on actual SEO work here than in any previous agency or in-house role.

What tools and systems do SEO experts use?

The stack varies by scope but commonly includes Google Search Console, GA4, Screaming Frog, Ahrefs, Semrush, and internal SOPs for quality control. The system provides shared templates for audits, keyword research, and reporting — so you are not rebuilding your methodology for every client. You bring your SEO judgment. The system provides the operational layer.

What is the salary range for freelance SEO experts at Vividigit?

There is no fixed SEO expert salary. Earnings are tied to revenue share — 40% of the revenue your work generates. An SEO expert working across multiple markets and scopes can earn significantly more than a comparable full-time SEO salary, because earnings scale with output and are not capped by salary bands or annual review cycles. On top of revenue share, 20% of company profit is distributed annually.

Is this a full-time SEO job or project-based?

Project-based by design. We match SEO experts to defined scopes and market-specific workstreams. Some roles grow into ongoing retainer relationships as the work expands. The model lets you work across multiple clients and markets simultaneously — more interesting and more lucrative than a single full-time SEO position.

Do I need to specialize in one country or language?

Strong specialization helps. General SEO capability matters, but the strongest fit is an expert who pairs the craft with real familiarity in a specific market — local SERPs, buyer language, competitive landscape, cultural nuance. If you are especially deep in one market or one type of SEO scope, that is often exactly what we need.

Can I apply if I do not match every listed market?

Yes. If your SEO expertise clearly maps to a market, language, or scope we work with — even if it is not listed yet — reach out. The system expands as new verticals and markets are added. Many of the strongest expert relationships started from introductions that did not match any open listing at the time.
